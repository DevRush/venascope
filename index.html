JVP Scope

See the pulse everyone squints for. This makes the jugular venous pulsation visible, proves it's venous — not carotid — and gauges how high the column sits.

  1. Frame the neck. Right side, just above the collarbone. Recline ~45°, head turned slightly left.
  2. Hold still. We magnify a sub-millimeter pulsation and lock onto the jugular column.
  3. Read it. Venous vs. carotid, the waveform, and the column height.
Identity venous vs. carotid — from ~180° phase and a ~400 ms carotid lag.
Waveform the extracted venous pulsation against the arterial reference.
Column height JVP above the sternal angle — an illustrative estimate.

Proof of concept — the signal pipeline is real; the height is an illustrative estimate, not for clinical use.
No video ever leaves your device. Assumes sinus rhythm, ~45° recline, and a fixed anatomical scale; green-channel signal is lighting- and skin-tone-sensitive.
